Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Ingredients

  • 100g pancetta (guanciale as a traditional alternative)
  • 350g spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese (Parmesan as a substitute)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and then the spaghetti. Cook according to package instructions until al dente, usually about 9 minutes.
  2. While the pasta cooks, cut the pancetta into small pieces or strips and place them in a skillet over medium heat. Cook until the fat renders and the pancetta becomes crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, beat the eggs and mix in the grated Pecorino Romano cheese.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta cooking water. Add the hot spaghetti directly to the skillet with the pancetta (heat off). Toss well to coat the pasta in the pancetta fat.
  5. Pour the egg and cheese mixture onto the pasta, tossing continuously. Add a little reserved pasta water if the sauce is too thick.
  6. Season generously with freshly ground black pepper and serve immediately.

Glossary

Pancetta
An Italian bacon made of pork belly meat, salt cured and spiced with black pepper.
Pecorino Romano Cheese
A hard, salty Italian cheese made from sheep's milk, traditionally used in Roman cuisine.
